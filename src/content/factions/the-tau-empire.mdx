## Army Rules

##### For the Greater Good


Each time a friendly <span class="keyword" data-term="tau" data-type="keyword">T’au</span> unit is the target of an enemy’s <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> Action, the closest friendly <span class="keyword" data-term="tau" data-type="keyword">T’au</span> unit (measured in Inches) that has not already been Activated during the current Battle Round can use this ability. To do so, Activate the unit after the <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> has been declared, but before any enemy models resolve their Charge moves. The Activated <span class="keyword" data-term="tau" data-type="keyword">T’au</span> unit can spend 1 of its Activation points to complete a <span class="keyword" data-term="shoot" data-type="action">Shoot</span> or <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> Action targeting the charging enemy unit only. Once the selected Action has been completed, the enemy unit can then complete its <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> Action. Each <span class="keyword" data-term="tau" data-type="keyword">T’au</span> unit can only use this ability once per Battle Round, and reduce their total Activation Points by 1 (to account for the Activation Point spent through this ability) when they are Activated normally at a later time.


##### T'au Battle Tactics


Before the Deployment step of the battle, you can use this ability if your army includes a Shas’O or Shas-vre. To do so, declare which of the following T’au Battle Tactics will be employed by your army. The selected Battle Tactic is in effect for the entire battle and cannot be changed for any reason. 


###### Mont’ka


Each time a T’au unit is Activated, select 1 of the following abilities to be in effect for the remainder of the battle round:
- The unit can <span class="keyword" data-term="shoot" data-type="action">Shoot</span> twice as its first 2 Actions, but reduce Hit rolls by 1 for the second <span class="keyword" data-term="shoot" data-type="action">Shoot</span> action. Further, the unit cannot <span class="keyword" data-term="move" data-type="action">Move</span> for the remainder of the Battle Round.
- The unit can <span class="keyword" data-term="move" data-type="action">Move</span> twice as its first 2 Actions, but neither can include an <span class="keyword" data-term="advance" data-type="action">Advance</span>, and the second <span class="keyword" data-term="move" data-type="action">Move</span> can only be a maximum distance equal to half the lowest Movement characteristic in the unit. Further, the unit cannot <span class="keyword" data-term="shoot" data-type="action">Shoot</span> or <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> for the remainder of the Battle Round.

###### Kauyon


T’au units possess the <span class="keyword" data-term="stealth" data-type="keyword">Stealth</span> ability until the first time they <span class="keyword" data-term="move" data-type="action">Move</span> or <span class="keyword" data-term="shoot" data-type="action">Shoot</span> when wholly positioned within a terrain feature. Units that already possess the Stealth ability increase the range of that ability from 12” to 24”. When a unit first <span class="keyword" data-term="shoots" data-type="action">Shoots</span> as part of its Activation, it can choose 1 of the following abilities to be in effect for that activation:
- The unit’s Ranged weapons gain the <span class="keyword" data-term="assault" data-type="keyword">Assault</span> ability. If Ranged weapons already possess the <span class="keyword" data-term="assault" data-type="keyword">Assault</span> ability, those weapons instead gain the <span class="keyword" data-term="sustained-hits-1" data-type="keyword">Sustained Hits 1</span> ability, or increase X by 1 if they already possess the <span class="keyword" data-term="sustained-hits-x" data-type="keyword">Sustained Hits [X]</span> ability.
- The unit’s Ranged weapons gain the <span class="keyword" data-term="precision" data-type="keyword">Precision</span> and <span class="keyword" data-term="lethal-hits" data-type="keyword">Lethal Hits</span> abilities, but:
- The unit must <span class="keyword" data-term="remain-stationary" data-type="action">Remain Stationary</span> as part of its Activation. 
- All models in the unit must target the same enemy unit. 
Ranged weapons that already possess the <span class="keyword" data-term="precision" data-type="keyword">Precision</span> ability instead score Critical Hits on a 4+.


##### “Our Plans Have Failed - Withdraw and Regroup!”


At the end of your Command Phase in the 3rd Battle Round, you must determine weather this ability will be in effect for the remainder of the battle. To do so, count the number of remaining friendly <span class="keyword" data-term="tau" data-type="keyword">T’au</span> units on the battlefield and in Strategic Reserves. If the number of units counted are less than half of the starting number of units in your army, this ability must take effect. 

If this ability is in effect, <span class="keyword" data-term="tau" data-type="keyword">T’au</span> units must attempt to Withdraw from the battlefield by moving off of it via a Deployment Edge. A Deployment Edge is any table edge within your Deployment Zone. To successfully Withdraw:
- All models in a unit currently on the battlefield must complete any moves towards the closest Deployment Edge with the goal of being removed from the battlefield as soon as possible.
- Units currently in Base-to-Base Contact must Fall Back and move towards the nearest friendly Deployment Edge, and complete subsequent moves that allow them to wholly cross the Deployment Edge and be removed from the battlefield as stated above.
- Units currently in Strategic Reserves cannot <span class="keyword" data-term="enter-the-battlefield-from-strategic-reserves" data-type="action">Enter the Battlefield from Strategic Reserves</span>.

Each <span class="keyword" data-term="tau" data-type="keyword">T’au</span> unit that successfully Withdraws in this manner is worth a number of Victory Points, as follows:
- <span class="keyword" data-term="battleline" data-type="keyword">Battleline</span> units: 3 Victory Points
- <span class="keyword" data-term="infantry" data-type="keyword">Infantry</span> units: 1 Victory Point
- <span class="keyword" data-term="vehicle" data-type="keyword">Vehicle</span> units: 2 Victory Points
- <span class="keyword" data-term="character" data-type="keyword">Character</span> units: 3 Victory Points

The enemy player can attempt to Route your units when they are attempting to Withdraw. Each friendly unit destroyed when this ability is triggered is considered to be Routed and is worth 2 Victory Points for your opponent.


## Wargear Upgrades

###### Markerlight	5 Points


A Markerlight is a Ranged weapon that generates zero damage but confers the following ability when fired:

**Target Acquisition:** Markerlights that successfully Hit an enemy unit provide stacking, cumulative abilities for other friendly T’au units that make Ranged attacks against that enemy unit:
- 1 to 5 Markerlight Hits: friendly T’au units add 1 to their Hit roll
- 6 to 10 Markerlight Hits: The above applies, and friendly T’au units increase their Ballistic Skill by 1
- 11 or more Markerlight Hits: The above applies, and friendly T’au ranged attacks (excluding attacks made by <span class="keyword" data-term="ordnance" data-type="keyword">Ordnance</span> or <span class="keyword" data-term="one-shot" data-type="keyword">One Shot</span> weapons) increase their Attacks characteristic by 1.

If an <span class="keyword" data-term="infantry" data-type="keyword">Infantry</span> model chooses to make a Ranged attack with a Markerlight, and that attack successfully Hits, it can only make that Ranged attack as part of its Activation; no other Ranged attacks can be made by that model.


###### Shield Generator	20 Points


The bearer is considered to have a Toughness characteristic of 6 against Ranged attacks, and gains the <span class="keyword" data-term="invulnerable-save-5" data-type="keyword">Invulnerable Save 5+</span> and <span class="keyword" data-term="bodyguard" data-type="keyword">Bodyguard</span> abilities.


###### Pulse Accelerator 	15 Points


Increase the Range characteristic by 6” and Armour Piercing characteristic by 1 for the following weapons when all models in the unit target the same enemy unit: Pulse Pistol, Pulse Carbine, Pulse Rifle, Long-shot Pulse Rifle, Pulse Cannon, Heavy Pulse Cannon.


###### Early Warning Override	20 Points


Enemy units cannot <span class="keyword" data-term="enter-the-battlefield-from-strategic-reserves" data-type="action">Enter the Battlefield from Strategic Reserves</span> within 12” of the bearer.


###### Counterfire Defence System	20 Points

When it does do, the Damage characteristic of enemy attacks is reduced by 1 (to a minimum of 1), and, if the bearer is still on the battlefield in your next Command Phase, it can regain up to D3 lost Wounds. 


###### Stealth Field Projector 	25 Points
