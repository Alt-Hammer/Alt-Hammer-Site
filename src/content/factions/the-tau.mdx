## Army Rules

##### For the Greater Good

Each time a friendly T'au unit is the target of an enemy's Charge and Fight Action, the closest friendly T'au unit (measured in Inches) that has not already been Activated during the current Battle Round can use this ability. To do so, Activate the unit after the Charge and Fight has been declared, but before any enemy models resolve their Charge moves. The Activated T'au unit can spend 1 of its Activation points to complete a Shoot or Charge and Fight Action targeting the charging enemy unit only. Once the selected Action has been completed, the enemy unit can then complete its Charge and Fight Action. Each T'au unit can only use this ability once per Battle Round, and reduce their total Activation Points by 1 (to account for the Activation Point spent through this ability) when they are Activated normally at a later time.

##### T'au Battle Tactics

Before the Deployment step of the battle, you can use this ability if your army includes a Shas'O or Shas-vre. To do so, declare which of the following T'au Battle Tactics will be employed by your army. The selected Battle Tactic is in effect for the entire battle and cannot be changed for any reason. 

###### Mont'ka

Each time a T'au unit is Activated, select 1 of the following abilities to be in effect for the remainder of the battle round:
- The unit can Shoot twice as its first 2 Actions, but reduce Hit rolls by 1 for the second Shoot action. Further, the unit cannot Move for the remainder of the Battle Round.
- The unit can Move twice as its first 2 Actions, but neither can include an Advance, and the second Move can only be a maximum distance equal to half the lowest Movement characteristic in the unit. Further, the unit cannot Shoot or Charge and Fight for the remainder of the Battle Round.

###### Kauyon

T'au units possess the Stealth ability until the first time they Move or Shoot when wholly positioned within a terrain feature. Units that already possess the Stealth ability increase the range of that ability from 12" to 24". When a unit first Shoots as part of its Activation, it can choose 1 of the following abilities to be in effect for that activation:
- The unit's Ranged weapons gain the Assault ability. If Ranged weapons already possess the Assault ability, those weapons instead gain the Sustained Hits 1 ability, or increase X by 1 if they already possess the Sustained Hits [X] ability.
- The unit's Ranged weapons gain the Precision and Lethal Hits abilities, but:
- The unit must Remain Stationary as part of its Activation. 
- All models in the unit must target the same enemy unit. 
Ranged weapons that already possess the Precision ability instead score Critical Hits on a 4+.






##### "Our Plans have Failed - Withdraw and Regroup!"

At the end of your Command Phase in the 3rd Battle Round, you must determine weather this ability will be in effect for the remainder of the battle. To do so, count the number of remaining friendly T'au units on the battlefield or in Strategic Reserves. If the number of units counted are less than half of the starting number of units in your army, this ability is considered to be in effect. 
If this ability is in effect, T'au units must attempt to Withdraw from the battlefield by moving off of it via a Deployment Edge. A Deployment Edge is any table edge within your Deployment Zone. To successfully Withdraw:
- All models in a unit currently on the battlefield must complete any moves towards the closest Deployment Edge with the goal of being removed from the battlefield as soon as possible.
- Units currently in Base-to-Base Contact must Fall Back and move towards the nearest friendly Deployment Edge, and complete subsequent moves that allow them to wholly cross the Deployment Edge and be removed from the battlefield as stated above.
- Units currently in Strategic Reserves cannot Enter the Battlefield from Strategic Reserves.

Each T'au unit that successfully Withdraws in this manner is worth a number of Victory Points, as follows:
- Battleline units: 3 Victory Points
- Infantry units: 1 Victory Point
- Vehicle units: 2 Victory Points
- Character units: 3 Victory Points

The enemy player can attempt to Route your units when they are attempting to Withdraw. Each friendly unit destroyed when this ability is triggered is considered to be Routed and is worth 2 Victory Points for your opponent.


## Wargear Upgrades

###### Markerlight	5 Points

A Markerlight is a Ranged weapon that generates zero damage but confers the following ability when fired:
**Target Acquisition: **Markerlights that successfully Hit an enemy unit provide stacking, cumulative abilities for other friendly T'au units that make Ranged attacks against that enemy unit:
- 1 to 5 Markerlight Hits: friendly T'au units add 1 to their Hit roll
- 6 to 10 Markerlight Hits: The above applies, and friendly T'au units increase their Ballistic Skill by 1
- 11 or more Markerlight Hits: The above applies, and friendly T'au ranged attacks (excluding attacks made by Ordnance or One Shot weapons) increase their Attacks characteristic by 1.

If an Infantry model chooses to make a Ranged attack with a Markerlight, and that attack successfully Hits, it can only make that Ranged attack as part of its Activation; no other Ranged attacks can be made by that model.

###### Shield Generator	20 Points

The bearer is considered to have a Toughness characteristic of 6 against Ranged attacks, and gains the Invulnerable Save 5+ and Bodyguard abilities.

###### Pulse Accelerator 	15 Points

Increase the Range characteristic by 6" and Armour Piercing characteristic by 1 for the following weapons when all models in the unit target the same enemy unit: Pulse Pistol, Pulse Carbine, Pulse Rifle, Long-shot Pulse Rifle, Pulse Cannon, Heavy Pulse Cannon.

###### Early Warning Override	20 Points

Enemy units cannot Enter the Battlefield from Strategic Reserves within 12" of the bearer.

###### Counterfire Defence System	20 Points

Reduce the Attacks characteristic of incoming Ranged attacks against the bearer's unit by D3 (to a minimum of 1).

###### Velocity Tracker	10 Points

Infantry Walker model only. The bearer gains the Anti Air 3+ ability when making Ranged attacks.

###### Multi-Tracker	10 Points

Infantry Walker model only. The bearer can make Ranged attacks as if it was a Vehicle.

###### Stim Injectors	25 Points

The bearer gains the Feel No Pain 5+ ability. Once per battle when the unit is the target of an enemy attack, you can choose to have the bearer drain its Stims. When it does do, the Damage characteristic of enemy attacks is reduced by 1 (to a minimum of 1), and, if the bearer is still on the battlefield in your next Command Phase, it can regain up to D3 lost Wounds. 

###### Stealth Field Projector 	25 Points

The unit gains the Stealth ability, or decreases the range of an existing Stealth ability from 12" to 6".

###### Target Lock	15 Points

Infantry Walker model only. The bearer's weapons gain the Assault ability. This wargear has no effect on weapons that already possess the Assault ability, or which possess the Ordnance keyword.

###### Advanced Targeting System	10 Points

Infantry Walker model only. The bearer scores Critical Wounds on a 5+ when making Ranged attacks.

###### Iridium Armour	15 Points

Infantry Walker model only. The bearer increases its Armour Save characteristic to 2+

###### XV85 Coldstar Battlesuit	15 Points

Infantry Walker model only. The bearer increases its Movement characteristic by 4", but reduces its Toughness characteristic by 1.

###### XV86 Enforcer Battlesuit	20 Points

Infantry Walker model only. The bearer increases its Toughness characteristic by 1 and its Wounds characteristic by 3, but reduces its Movement characteristic by 2".

###### Experimental Weapons System	10 Points per Weapons

- Ranged weapons increase their Armour Piercing and Damage characteristics by 1, but gain the Hazardous D3 ability.
- Ranged Torrent weapons instead increase their Strength and Attacks characteristic by 2 and gain the Poison 1 ability, but gain the Heavy ability reduce their Range characteristic by 3".

#### Character


##### Shas'O


###### Force Organization

Your army can include 1 Shas'O unit for 2,000 points.

###### Unit Composition

1 Shas'O

###### Standard Wargear

1 Crisis Battlesuit Hardpoint equipped with 1 of the following:
- Flamer
- Pulse Cannon
- Plasma Rifle
- Fusion Blaster
- Smart Missile Pod
- Airburst Fragmentation Launcher

###### Special Abilities

**Adaptive Strategist: **During your first Command Phase, after you have selected your T'au Battle Tactic for the battle, you can select up to 3 units currently on the battlefield and complete 1 of the following:
- If you selected Mont'ka, those 3 units gain the Scout 6" ability. If they already possess the Scout [X]" ability, increase [X] by 3".
- If you selected Kauyon, those 3 units can either be repositioned anywhere on the battlefield that allows the unit to be placed wholly within a terrain feature, or be placed into Strategic Reserves.

This unit cannot be selected as 1 of the 3 units you can select for this ability.
**Positional Relay: **Once per battle round, when a friendly T'au unit Enters the Battlefield From Strategic Reserves, it can do so within 9" of this unit, even if there are enemy models within 9" of this unit. When doing so, the arriving unit must be placed wholly within 9" of this unit, and cannot have any models placed within 1" of any enemy models.
