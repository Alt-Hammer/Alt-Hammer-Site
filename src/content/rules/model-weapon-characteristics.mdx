Every model and weapon in Alt-Hammer has a series of Characteristics that define its physical traits and gameplay capabilities. Characteristics are expressed as fixed numerical values, or as randomly-generated numerical values using one or more D6. In some cases, Characteristics use another Characteristic’s value (such as S+2, S+D6). ‘Base’ Characteristics are defined in the Data Tables for each Faction, which all include the Unit and Weapon Data Tables, and additional Characteristics - or modifications to Characteristics - are unique to Factions and often covered under Faction Index sections such as Wargear Upgrades and Special Abilities for a specific unit. Brief descriptions of each Characteristic in Alt-Hammer 40,000 are provided here.

## Model Characteristics


#### Movement (M)

Distance a model can move in Inches. Many model type provide additional rules on how a model is moved, and many wargear keywords can change or impact a model’s Movement characteristic as well.

#### Weapons Skill (WS)

The level of competency a model has with making and parrying attacks with Melee weapons, expressed as a fixed numerical value. Weapons Skill is used to determine Hit rolls for Melee attacks, which is a relational process determined by comparing the attacking models’ Weapons Skills, per the Hit Roll Table covered under the Making Attacks section.

#### Ballistics Skill (BS)

The level of competency a model has with making and dodging attacks with Ranged weapons, expressed as a fixed numerical value. Ballistics Skill is used to determine Hit rolls for Ranged attacks, which is a relational process determined by comparing the attacking models’ Ballistics Skills, per the Hit Roll Table covered under the Making Attacks section.

#### Initiative (I)

The level of speed, agility and aggression of a model. Initiative is used for a variety of purposes in Alt-Hammer, including:
1. How far a model can <span class="keyword" data-term="advance" data-type="action">Advance</span> as part of a <span class="keyword" data-term="move" data-type="action">Move</span> Action, wherein Initiative becomes the Advance distance expressed in Inches
2. Determining how far a model can Charge as part of a <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> Action, wherein Initiative is used to determine the total Charge distance in inches.
3. Determining how far a model can Pile In as part of the <span class="keyword" data-term="fight" data-type="action">Fight</span> Action.
4. Determining the sequence of Fights between various models, where those with higher Initiative characteristics will get to <span class="keyword" data-term="fight" data-type="action">Fight</span> before models with lower Initiative characteristics

#### Attacks

Attacks represent the offensive capabilities of a model; how many times it can strike. A model’s Attacks characteristic is most commonly used to determine the effective amount of Attacks a Melee weapon has, but some other use cases may also apply. 

#### Strength (S)

The physical might of a model, expressed in a fixed numerical value. Many Melee weapons - and some Ranged weapons - also use this value to determine their own Strength characteristic. 

#### Toughness (T)

A model’s ability to withstand or shrug off attacks, expressed as a fixed numerical value. Toughness is used to determine whether an attack’s Strength is sufficient to successfully wound a target using the Wound Roll Table in the Making Attacks section.

#### Wounds (W)

Wounds are measured in fixed numerical values and represent the amount of Damage needed from attacks that successfully Wound that model before it is considered Destroyed (and, in most cases, permanently removed from the battlefield). 

#### Armour Save (Sv)

Armour Saves represent the effectiveness of a model’s protective wargear - or, in some cases, their lack thereof. A model’s Armour Save characteristic is expressed as a D6 value that must be met or exceeded by a model in order to avoid suffering Damage from successful Wounds. When a model fails to roll the effective Armour Save needed, an attack’s Damage characteristic is applied to that model.
Armour Saves are typically modified by external variables such as attack Armour Piercing characteristics, Terrain Attributes, and unit or faction Abilities. Players should take note of the cumulative effects of these modifiers before making their Armour Save rolls.

#### Leadership (Ld)

Leadership characteristics represent a model’s bravery and resilience, and for <span class="keyword" data-term="psykers" data-type="keyword">Psykers</span>, their ability to muster Psychic abilities and attacks. Leadership characteristics are expressed as a 2D6 value that must be met or exceeded by a model in order to successfully pass tests that use this characteristic. The most common such tests are Battle Shock tests and Psychic Tests, but other unit or faction Abilities may also prompt units (or models) to pass similar tests using this Leadership characteristic.

#### Points (Pts)

Points are a numerical value that represent the ‘cost’ of an individual model for the purposes of army list building and game size determination. 

## Weapon Characteristics


#### Points (Pts)

Points are a numerical value that represent the ‘cost’ of an individual weapon for the purposes of army list building and game size determination. 

#### Range (Rng.)

Range is the distance in inches within which a weapon’s attacks can be allocated to eligible target enemy units. Ranged weapons typically only have values, while Melee weapons typically have a value of “-“ which represents 0” of Range, indicating that the weapon can only be used against eligible targets in a <span class="keyword" data-term="fight" data-type="action">Fight</span> Action. Some weapons such as Grenades have a Range characteristic that can vary based on the attacking model’s Strength plus an additional value measured in Inches - ie “S+3”. In that example, if the attacking model has a Strength characteristic, the effective strength of that Ranged weapon would be 5” (2”+3”). 

#### Attacks (A)

Attacks represent the offensive capabilities of a weapon; how many times it can strike, shoot, etc. A weapon’s Attacks characteristic can be randomly determined (ie D6+1), and is often affected by modifiers from keywords and abilities. Some weapons have multiple ‘profiles’ that the player must choose 1 from before making attacks with the weapon. Many weapons - mainly Melee weapons - have model-derived Attacks values - typically shown as A+2 or similar. If a model with an Attacks characteristic of 2 is making attacks with a weapon that has an Attacks characteristics of A+2, then they would make 4 Attacks in total with weapon (2+2).

#### Armour Piercing (AP)

Armour Piercing represents a weapon’s ability to pierce a target’s protective armour. Armour Piercing is represented as a negative numerical value (ie, -1), which is subtracted from a target model’s Armour Save characteristic when it is forced to roll an Armour Save. For example, if you successfully Wound a model with an Armour Save characteristic of 3+ using a weapon with an Armour Piercing characteristic of -1, the target model’s effective Armour Save characteristic would be reduced to 4+ (3+, -1) - a harder Armour Save roll to pass.

#### Damage (D)

A weapon’s Damage characteristic is a numerical value that represents how many Wounds a model suffers when the weapon’s attacks are successful. Damage values are typically fixed values, but many weapons have randomly determined Damage characteristics as well, typically shown as one or more D6 (ie 2D6). Sometimes, weapons will have a combination of random D6 value plus a fixed additive value (ie 2D6+3). In those instances, the player rolls the specified number of dice, determined their value, and then adds the fixed numerical value on top of that to create the cumulative Damage characteristic of that successful attack.

#### Weapon Availability for Armour & Wargear

Some factions - most notably factions with the Astartes keyword - possess various armour and wargear that limit (or expand) the weapons and wargear options available to models per that unit’s Unit Profile. Armour and wargear keywords listed in this section of a weapon’s data profile mean that the weapon is eligible to be selected by models that possess any of those armour and/or wargear keywords, and not eligible to be selected by models that do not possess any of those keywords.
