The main way your units and their wargear are characterized is through Keywords. These keywords define standard attributes and capabilities for that unit, its models, and the wargear they can be equipped with, and include: basic functions and abilities, terrain interactions and limitations that must be adhered to, and in some cases force organization requirements. Virtually all Keywords are universal to all factions, units and wargear in Alt-Hammer, and are defined here.

## Model Characteristics


### General Gameplay Characteristics

All models in Alt-Hammer must abide by the following core limitations to how they function on the tabletop:
1. A model cannot be moved over another model's base, or any part of the model itself if the model does not have a base. This does not apply to models with any of the following keywords: Titanic, Aircraft, Fly, or Jump Pack.
2. A model can be equipped with multiple Ranged weapons, but can only make Ranged attacks with 1 of those Ranged weapons as part of a Shoot Action. Some model type keywords supersede this standard limitation.
3. A model can be equipped with multiple Melee weapons, but can only make Melee attacks with 1 of those Melee weapons as part of a Charge and Fight* *or Fight Action. This limitation does not apply to Melee weapons that have the Extra Attacks ability equipped by that model.

### Core Model Type Keywords

All Models in Alt-Hammer will have 1 of the following Model Type Keywords, which provide specific rules for how that model can interact with other models, make attacks, and the tabletop and terrain.

#### Infantry

Infantry models must abide by the following when completing the Move or Charge and Fight* *Actions:
1. Treat any terrain feature that is less than 1" tall as if it was not there. However, moving over terrain features that are 1" or taller must be factored into the model's movement as vertical distance it must travel.
2. Reduce their Movement characteristic by 2" when entering or exiting a terrain feature with the Building keyword. This is to represent models spending their energy finding safe entry points breaching through walls, or clambering over debris. Move* *Actions that begin and end within a terrain feature with the Building keyword are not subject to this constraint, but are subject to vertical terrain feature measuring detailed above.

When Infantry models make Ranged attacks, they can choose to target more than 1 enemy unit if they possess more than 1 Ranged weapon. However, the attacking player can only choose 1 target to attack at the model's full Ballistic Skill. Ranged attacks against each additional unit targeted reduces the model's Ballistic Skill by 1. These reductions are applied in the order that the player chooses to make their attacks (i.e., the first weapon fired will have full Ballistic Skill, the second will have Ballistic Skill -1, the third will have Ballistic Skill -2, and so on). 


#### Vehicle

Vehicles must abide by the following when completing a Move Action:
1. The model must always move forwards or backwards relative to the front of the vehicle's hull (use reasonable logic to identify this based on the design of the vehicle!). When completing any form of Movement, a Vehicle must decide whether it will turn or pivot as part of its movement, or go in a straight line relative to the front of the model's hull. If the model will turn or pivot, reduce its Movement characteristic by 2".
2. The model cannot move over any terrain features that are more than 2" tall relative to the ground.
3. The model may only enter terrain by completing a Breach test. To complete a Breach test, roll a D6. On a 3+ the Vehicle successfully breaches the terrain and can be moved into or through that terrain feature. Vehicles with a Toughness characteristic of 12 or above reduce the Breach test to 2+.
4. Vehicles moving into or through a terrain feature reduce their Movement characteristic by 2". This is additional over any reductions to the Movement characteristic associated with turning or pivoting, as detailed above.

Vehicles can make Ranged attacks with any number of Ranged weapons they are equipped with and may target any enemy units as chosen by the attacking player. They also ignore the effects of the Heavy keyword when making attacks. 
Vehicles that are reduced to 1/3 of their Starting Strength (rounding down) reduce their Activation Points by 1 and subtract 1 from their Hit rolls.

#### Monster

Monster models must abide by the following when completing a Move Action:
1. The model may only enter terrain by completing a Breach test. To complete a Breach test, roll a D6. On a 3+ the Monster successfully breaches the terrain and can be moved into or through that terrain feature. Monsters with a Toughness characteristic of 12 or above reduce the Breach test to 2+.
2. Monsters moving into or through a terrain feature reduce their Movement characteristic by 2". 
3. Cannot move more than 4" vertically through any terrain feature.

Monsters can make Ranged attacks with any number of Ranged weapons they are equipped with and may target any enemy units as chosen by the attacking player. They also ignore the effects of the Heavy keyword when making attacks..
Monsters that are reduced to 1/3 of their Starting Strength (rounding down) reduce their Activation Points by 1 and subtract 1 from their Hit rolls.

#### Beast

Models with the Beast keyword: 
1. Have 2 Activation Points instead of 3.
2. Always possess the Relentless ability. 
3. Must abide by the following when completing a Move Action:
4. Cannot enter terrain features with the Building keyword.
5. Cannot move more than 4" vertically through any terrain feature.
6. Cannot complete the Mission Task Action.

### Additional Model Type Keywords

The following model type keywords can be found on any model in Alt-Hammer. These keywords provide further rules for how that model can interact with other models, make attacks, and the tabletop and terrain that may, in some instances, overwrite the core model type keywords that model possesses. These keywords can also impact force organization, provide pre-game abilities, and abilities used throughout the battle.

#### Battleline

Up to 2 of each unit with the Battleline keyword can be taken in your army for every 500 points. Battleline units are also eligible to make Bonus Activations, may be eligible for other Mission-specific rules, and often have interactions with faction-specific Army Rules and abilities.

#### Character

Up to 1 of each unit with the Character keyword can be taken in your army for every 1,000 points. 
Models with the Character keyword can only be selected as the subject of an attack under the following conditions:
1. The model is the closest model to the attacking unit when making a Ranged attack, and is not within 3" of any other friendly Units.
2. The model is in base-to-base contact with the attacking model when making a Melee attack.
3. The attacking model has the Precision ability.

The conditions above do not apply to Character units that also possess any of the following model type keywords: Vehicle, Monster.

#### Leader

Models with the Leader keyword can choose to lead a friendly Unit. The Units that the Leader is eligible to lead are listed on its Unit Profile. Leader models and friendly Units they are leading is determined during your Pregame Setup, before the units are placed on the battlefield, and before the decision to declare that those units are either embarked in a Transport, or placed into Strategic Reserve. Leader models and the Unit they are leading count as a single unit for Activation purposes, and when an enemy unit selects them as the subject of an attack.

#### Bodyguard

When a model or unit with the Bodyguard keyword is being led by a Leader unit with the Character keyword, Damage that would be inflicted on that Character unit must be allocated to Bodyguard model(s) instead.

#### Walker

Models with the Walker keyword must abide by the following when completing a Move Action:
1. Do not need to reduce their Movement characteristic when making a turn or a pivot.
2. The model may only enter terrain by completing a Breach test. To complete a Breach test, roll a D6. On a 2+ the Walker successfully breaches the terrain and can be moved into or through that terrain feature. 
3. Cannot move more than 4" vertically through any terrain feature.

Walkers ignore the effects of the Heavy keyword when making attacks. 

#### Mounted

Models with the Mounted keyword must abide by the following when completing a Move Action:
1. The model must always move forwards relative to the front of the model's body (typically identified by the direction its head is facing, or equivalent feature such as hull front). 
2. When completing any form of Movement, a Mounted model must decide whether it will turn or pivot as part of its movement, or go in a straight line relative to the front of the model's body. If the model will turn or pivot, reduce its Movement characteristic by 2".
3. Cannot enter terrain features with the Building keyword.
4. Cannot move more than 4" vertically through any terrain feature.

#### Swarm

Models with the Swarm keyword: 
1. Have 2 Activation Points instead of 3.
2. Cannot move more than 1" vertically through any terrain feature.
3. Cannot complete the Mission Task Action.

#### Psyker

Psykers can access Psychic weapons available to their respective Faction, and typically possess unique psychic abilities. The specifics of each's Psyker's rules are covered in their Unit Profile.

#### Small Profile

Small Profile models add 1 to their Toughness characteristic when they are the target of a Ranged attack. Additionally, these models gain the Invulnerable Save 4+ ability against Ranged attacks when wholly positioned in a terrain feature with the Light Cover or Heavy Cover keywords.

#### Phase Shift

Models with this ability can move horizontally through any model or Terrain Feature as if it was not there.

#### Hover

Models with the Hover keyword treat any terrain feature that is less than 4" tall as if it was not there and are not required to reduce their Movement characteristic when making a Move* *action that includes a turn or pivot if the model also has other Model Type keywords (such as Vehicle, Monster, and Mounted) that would require it to do so. Any other restrictions to Move actions determined by other keywords the model has are still in effect.

#### Jump Pack

When completing either a Move or Charge and Fight Action, models with the Jump Pack keyword can ignore any terrain features that are less than 8" tall.

#### Fly

When completing either a Move or Charge and Fight Action, models with the Fly keyword:
1. Can ignore any terrain features that are less than 8" tall.
2. Do not need to make a Breaching Test to enter terrain features with the Building keyword if another keyword the model possesses typically requires that they do so (ie, Vehicle or Monster).
3. Are not required to reduce their Movement characteristic when making a Move* *action that includes a turn or pivot if the model also has other Model Type keywords (such as Vehicle, Monster, and Mounted) that would require it to do so.

Further, enemy units making a Ranged attack against a model with the Fly keyword reduce their Hit rolls by 1, unless that model has not moved this Battle Round, or moved less than 16" this Battle Round as part of a Move Action.

#### Aircraft

Models with the Aircraft keyword have two values in their Movement characteristic: [X]"-[Y]". [X]" is the minimum value this model must* *move, and [Y]" is the maximum value this model can move.
Additionally, models with the Aircraft keyword:
1. Cannot elect to Remain Stationary when it is Activated, unless the model also possesses the Hover keyword.
2. Are not required to reduce their Movement characteristic when making a Move action that includes a turn or pivot (a limitation that is imposed by other Model Type keywords such as Vehicle, Monster). However, an Aircraft cannot turn or pivot more than 90 degrees in a single move unless the following 2 conditions are met:
3. The model possesses the Hover keyword.
4. The model moved more than 16" 
5. Are always treated as being fully visible to attacking enemy units making Ranged attacks, even if the Aircraft's position on the board is physically obscured. 
6. Are always able to draw line of sight to an enemy unit they target for a Ranged attack. When making these attacks, weapons gain the Ignores Cover ability.
7. Can never benefit from Light Cover or Heavy Cover.
8. Cannot be the target of an enemy unit's Charge and Fight* *Action unless that enemy unit has the Fly or Jump Pack keywords.
9. Cannot use the following Actions: Fight* *and Charge and Fight*.*

Further, enemy units making a Ranged attack against an Aircraft reduce their Hit rolls by 1, unless that Aircraft model moved less than 16" this Battle Round.

#### Titanic

Model with the Titanic keyword:
1. Can never benefit from Light Cover or Heavy Cover.
2. Cannot benefit from the Obscuring keyword while any part of the model is visible to an attacking enemy model using true line of sight. 
3. Treat the Ordnance wargear ability as if it was the Heavy wargear ability when making Ranged attacks.
4. Can move through terrain features unless that terrain feature has the Impassable Terrain keyword. When they do so, reduce their Movement characteristic by 4". After a model with the Titanic keyword has moved through a terrain feature, remove that terrain feature from the tabletop and replace it with a terrain feature (or area marker) that represents Difficult Ground only, unless the terrain feature has the Hill keyword. This is to reflect the total destructive impact of such a large machine or creature traversing any physical structures, vegetation, or other objects. (Remember that terrain features with the Impassable Terrain keyword cannot be moved through or over unless a model has the Aircraft or Fly keywords!) 
5. Can move over and passed enemy and friendly models as if they were not there. Additionally, when making a move passed models, or ending a move within Base-to-base contact of a model (ie, as part of a Charge and Fight Action), roll a D6 for each such model. On a 5+ that model suffers a Mortal Wound. This represents that model - friend, or foe - suffering the consequences of being unable to get out of the way of the Titanic model in time!

#### Infiltrator

Units where every model possesses the Infiltrator keyword can be set up anywhere in No Man's Land that is at least 9" horizontally or diagonally from the edge of the enemy deployment zone that borders No Man's Land, and at least 9" horizontally and/or vertically from any enemy units already deployed using this ability. 
Additionally, models with this ability ignore modifiers to their Movement characteristic when moving through Light Cover or Heavy Cover.

#### Deep Strike

Units where every model possesses the Deep Strike keyword can Enter the Battlefield from Strategic Reserve as detailed in the Core Activations section. However, these units can be placed anywhere on the battlefield that is at least 9" horizontally and/or vertically from any enemy units.

#### Scout [X]"

Units where every model possesses the Scouts keyword can complete a Normal Move up to [X]" before the start of the Activation Phase in Battle Round 1. If each player have units with this ability, the player with the Initiative resolves the first Scout move, and players alternate through any remaining units with Scout moves thereafter.

#### Stealth

Units with the Stealth ability cannot be the subject of a Range attack if the attacking model is more than 12" away. 

#### Unyielding

Units with the Unyielding keyword can use the Charge and Fight Action in an activation that began with a Fall Back. 

#### Relentless

Units with the Relentless keyword can use the Charge and Fight Action in an activation that began with a Move action that included an advance*.*

#### Fearless

Units with the Fearless keyword never need to take a Battle Shock test.

#### Transport [X]

Models with the Transport [X] keyword can transport a number of friendly Infantry models up to the value of X. Note that some units, models or wargear may identify that a model takes the place of more than 1 model for transport purposes. Further, models with the following keywords can never embark in a model with the Transport [X] ability: Fly, Jump Pack, Hover, Mounted, Vehicle, Walker, Swarm.

#### Firing Deck [X]

Models with the Firing Deck [X] ability are models that have the Transport [X] ability and feature hatches, ports or open-topped designs that enable embarked models to make Ranged attacks. The [X] in Transport [X] identifies how many embarked models can make Ranged attacks out of the Transport model as part of their unit's activation. Units, models or wargear that identify that a model takes the place of more than 1 model for transport purposes also count as that many models for the purposes of this ability as well.

#### Deadly Demise [X]

When a model with the Deadly Demise [X] ability is destroyed, roll a D6. On a roll of a 1 or a 6, this ability takes effect. When it does so, each unit with at least 1 model within 6" of this unit suffers [X] Mortal Wounds.

## Armour Save Abilities

Many units and models possess enhanced protective abilities in addition to the Armour Save characteristic identified on their profile either through unique wargear they can acquire, or their own extraordinary physical properties. These protective abilities take the form of the following keywords.

#### Invulnerable Save [X]+

Models with this ability possess an Invulnerable Save of [X]+, where [X] is the maximum value that their Armour Save characteristic can be reduced to. For example, a model that has a base Armour Save characteristic of 3+ is the target of an attack that has an Armour Piercing value of -3, which would reduce their Armour Save characteristic to 6+. However, the model also possesses the Invulnerable Save 4+ ability, and therefor the Armour Piercing value of the attack is superseded by this ability, allowing the model to make its Armour Save on a 4+ instead of a 6+.

#### Feel No Pain [X]+

Models with this ability possess an additional saving throw made against each point of Damage inflicted on them by an enemy's attack, where the value of [X]+ determines whether that point of Damage is taken by the model with this ability. For example, a model with a Wounds characteristic of 4 and the Feel No Pain 6+ ability is successfully wounded by an attack with a Damage characteristic of 3. The model rolls 3 dice - one for each point of Damage - and rolls a 2, 5 and 6. This means the model successfully avoided 1 of the 3 points of Damage from that attack with the roll of a 6, resulting in only 2 Damage taken and a loss of 2 of its 4 Wounds.

## Wargear Abilities


#### Pistol

Units are eligible to make a Ranged attack using a weapon with this ability even if they Advanced as part of a Move Action or are in Base-to-base contact with enemy models. 

#### Assault

Units are eligible to make a Ranged attack with this weapon even if they Advanced as part of a Move Action.

#### Heavy

Infantry models making attacks with Heavy weapons do so as follows:
For Ranged attacks: 
1. Reduce Hit rolls by 1 and Initiative characteristic by 1 if their unit has already Moved. 
2. Models that Move after making Ranged attacks reduce their Movement characteristic by half (rounding down), cannot Advance as part of that Move Action, and reduce their Initiative characteristic by 1. 

For Melee attacks:
1. Reduce Hit rolls by 1 and Initiative characteristic by 1 if their unit has already Moved. 

These effects last until the end of the Battle Round.

#### Rapid Fire [X]

Weapons with this ability increase their Attacks characteristic by [X] when making Ranged attacks at enemy models that are half of the weapon's Range characteristic or less from the bearer.

#### Grenade

A maximum of 1 model in every 5 models in a unit can make a Ranged attack with a Grenade type weapon as part of a Shoot Action.

#### Torrent

Models making a Ranged attack using a weapon with the Torrent keyword automatically Hit their target.

#### Precision

Models making an attack using a weapon with the Precision keyword always choose a specific enemy model - including models with the Character keyword - as their target. All other criteria for a model being an eligible target (ie, line of sight) apply to this attack.

#### Ordnance

Models are only eligible to make a Ranged attack using a weapon with the Ordnance keyword if they first Remained Stationary this Activation, unless the model possesses the Titanic keyword. Instead, Models with the Titanic keyword:
1. Reduce Hit rolls by 1 and Initiative characteristic by 1 if their unit has already Moved. 
2. Reduce their Movement characteristic by half (rounding down) if they Move after making Ranged attacks, and cannot Advance as part of that Move Action, and reduce their Initiative characteristic by 1.

#### Indirect Fire

Models can target enemy units that it cannot draw line of sight to using weapons with this keyword. When doing so, the enemy units are considered to have Light Cover. Indirect Fire attacks can never benefit from the Precision ability under any circumstances. 

#### Blast [X]"

Weapons with the Blast [X]" ability always include some amount of randomly generated attacks (in the form of D6, 2D6, D3+3, etc.) as identified in the weapon's Attacks characteristic, where [X]" determines the weapon's Blast radius. Models making a Ranged attack using a weapon with the Blast [X]" keyword do so as follows:
1. **Place the Blast Marker:** After selecting a weapon with the Blast [X]" keyword, place a round 25mm / ~1" marker anywhere within range and line of sight of the attacking model. The marker can be placed over enemy models, including those in base-to-base contact with friendly models.
2. **Determine Units Hit:** Identify all units (friendly and enemy) with at least one model wholly or partially within the Blast radius.
3. **Allocate Attacks:** Determine the number of attacks as indicated by the weapon's profile (e.g., D6, 2D6, D3+3, etc.). For every 5 models within the Blast radius, add 1 additional attack to the total pool.
4. **Assign Attacks to Units:** Divide the total number of attacks as evenly as possible among all units within the Blast radius. Odd, additional attacks - ie, the seventh attack out of a total of 7, for example - are assigned to the unit with the most models within the Blast radius.
5. **Resolve ****Attack Sequence for Each ****Unit****:** For each unit affected, resolve the attack sequence as normal, applying any modifiers to that specific unit from abilities affecting that unit. 

**Note on Interactions between** Blast** & **Stealth**:** Blast attacks can only be allocated to enemy units benefitting from the Stealth ability if other enemy units are within the Blast radius. This is to represent your units not wasting their shots on areas where they can't identify any targets.

#### Beam [X]

Models making a Ranged attack using a weapon with the Beam [X] keyword do so as follows:
1. Select the target unit for the attack.
2. Roll to Hit.
3. If the Hit is successful, use a 1" wide straight edge (ie, a tape measure, or similar) to draw a straight line between the bearer's base to any visible part of the target unit. 
4. Identify any units - both yours and your opponents - with at least 1 model within 1" of the straight line. Each unit that meets the above criteria suffers [X] Mortal Wounds.
5. Complete the attack sequence against the target unit of the attack as normal.

Disregard steps 3 and 4 if you are making an attack with a Beam [X] weapon against a unit with the Aircraft or Fly keywords.

#### Anti-Air

Models making an attack using a weapon with the Anti-Air ability increase their Ballistic Skill by 1 and count every Hit as a Critical Hit against enemy units that possess the Aircraft or Fly keywords.

#### Sustained Hits [X]

Models making an attack using a weapon with the Sustained Hits [X] ability add [X] additional Hits for each Critical Hit scored. These [X] additional Hits do not count as Critical Hits.

#### Lethal Hits

Attacks made with the Lethal Hits ability increase their Armour Penetration characteristic by 1 when that attack scores a Critical Hit. 

#### Lance

Add 1 to the Wound roll each time a Melee attack is made using a weapon that has the Lance ability if the bearer attacked with this weapon as part of a Charge and Fight Action.

#### Anti-[KEYWORD] [X]+

Models making an attack using a weapon with the Anti-[Keyword] [X] ability score a Critical Wound on [X]+ against enemy units that possess the corresponding [Keyword].

#### Devastating Wounds

Models making an attack that scores a Critical Wound using a weapon with the Devastating Wounds keyword inflict a number of Mortal Wounds equal to the Damage characteristic of the attack.

#### Hazardous [X]

Models that roll a 1 to Hit suffer [X] Mortal Wounds when making attacks using a weapon with the Hazardous ability.

#### Poison [X]

Models (excluding those with the Vehicle keyword) suffer additional Damage equal to [X] on a Critical Wound from attacks made with weapons that possess the Poison keyword.

#### Melta [X]

A ranged attack using a weapon with the Melta [X] keyword adds [X] to that attack's Strength and Damage characteristics if the positions of the bearer and target enemy unit meet one of the following conditions:
1. The bearer is within half of the weapon's Range characteristic or less from the target enemy unit, or,
2. The bearer is within 9" of the target enemy unit.

#### Harpoon

Models Hit by 1 or more Harpoon weapons:
1. Reduce their Activation Points by 1
2. Must remain within a distance equal to the Harpoon weapon's Range from the attacking model until:
3. The attacking model uses the Harpoon weapon again as part of a subsequent attack, or,
4. The attacking model makes a subsequent attack of any kind.

Additionally, models Hit by 1 or more Harpoon weapons can *drag* attacking models, or be themselves *dragged* by those attacking models. This works as follows:
1. If the target's Toughness is lower than the attacking model's Strength, the attacker can *drag* the target when they move, but the target must stay within the Harpoon weapon's Range.
2. If the target's Toughness is equal to or higher than the attacking model's Strength, the target can *drag* the attacker when they move, but the attacker must stay within the Harpoon weapon's Range.

In instances where multiple Harpoon weapons have successfully Hit a single model, then use the cumulative Strength of all attacking models to determine which of the above scenarios applies.
Further, Models Hit by 1 or more Harpoon weapons can complete a Charge and Fight Action against any enemy units. However, all of the above restrictions must be observed when resolving that Charge and Fight Action.

#### Ignores Cover

Enemy units that must make an Armour Save from attacks with the Ignores Cover ability never receive the benefits provided from Light Cover or Dense Cover.

#### Ensnare

Units successfully hit by a weapon with the Ensnare keyword reduce their Activation Points by 1 for the remainder of the Battle Round in addition to any other effects or damage inflicted by the weapon. These weapons have no effect on models with the Vehicle keyword. 

#### Haywire

Vehicle models successfully hit by Haywire weapons reduce their Activation Points by 1 for the remainder of the Battle Round in addition to any other effects or damage inflicted by the weapon.

#### Extra Attacks

Models equipped with Melee weapons that have the Extra Attacks ability can make Melee attacks with any such weapons in addition to Melee attacks with a weapon that does not have this ability.

#### Twin-Linked

The Twin-linked ability is never found on a weapon's profile, but instead can be added to a weapon's profile by units that offer this ability as part of their wargear options in the form of paired weapons. For example, some units may allow models to be equipped with Twin-Linked Heavy Bolters, or Twin-Linked Lightning Claws, etc. as an option among potentially others. The Twin-Linked ability makes the following modifications to a weapon's profile: 
1. Increase the Attacks characteristic by 1.
2. Grants the Sustained Hits 1 ability, or, if the paired weapons already have the Sustained Hits [X] ability, then the value of [X] for it is increased by 1.
3. If the weapon cannot have the Sustained Hits [X] ability because it is not required to make Hit rolls (ie, because it has the Torrent keyword), then the weapon's Attacks characteristic is increased by 3 instead of 1.

For example, a Twin-Linked Heavy Bolter will have 1 more attack (for a total of 4) than a single Heavy Bolter, and would have the Sustained Hits 2 ability (adding 1 to the Sustained Hits 1 ability a Heavy Bolter has as standard).
The Twin-linked ability is accessed via a Faction's Wargear Upgrades and uses a variable points cost system based on various weapon characteristics.

#### Smoke [X]"

Smoke is a special form of blast attack that provides an area effect instead of damage. To complete an attack using a weapon or wargear with the smoke ability:
1. **Place the ****Smoke ****Marker:** Place a round 25mm / ~1" marker anywhere within range and line of sight of the attacking model. The marker can be placed over enemy models, including those in base-to-base contact with friendly models. 
2. Leave this marker in place until the end of the Battle Round. The effect of the smoke attack lasts until the end of the Battle Round.
3. Do not roll to Hit. Smoke attacks do not incur Damage of any kind, and thus no further attack sequence actions apply once the smoke marker has been placed.

**For ****Units making ****Ranged Attacks**** near Smoke Markers****: **Each time a unit makes a Ranged attack that draws line of sight within [X]" of the smoke attack's marker, reduce the Ballistic Skill of that attack to 2.
**For Fights****:**
1. Each time a unit completes a Charge and Fight Action that involves moving some or all of its models within [X]" of the smoke marker, reduce the Initiative characteristic of those models by 2.
2. Each time a model that is positioned within [X]" of the smoke marker is selected to Fight, reduce Hit rolls by 1.

#### Combi Weapons

Combi-weapons combine 2 or more typical weapon profiles into a single piece of wargear. When making attacks, core model type keywords of the bearer (i.e. Infantry, Monster, Vehicle, etc.) must be observed.

## Terrain Attributes & Types


### Terrain Attribute Keywords

Every terrain feature used in an Alt-Hammer battle should possess at least 1 of the following Terrain Attribute keywords. Each of these keywords specifies how models interact with that terrain feature and what benefits or drawbacks that terrain feature may provide (ie, forms of cover).
Prior to starting your Alt-Hammer battle, players should establish which terrain features possess which of the following Terrain Attribute keywords.

#### Light Cover

1. Models making Ranged attacks against models wholly positioned in this terrain feature reduce their Hit roll by 1. This applies when both models are whole wholly positioned in this terrain feature as well.
2. Reduce the Armour Piercing characteristic of Ranged attacks by 1 against models wholly positioned in Light Cover.

#### Heavy Cover

1. Models making Ranged attacks against models wholly positioned in Heavy Cover - or against models wholly positioned behind Heavy Cover - reduce their Hit roll by 1.
2. Reduce the Armour Piercing characteristic of Ranged attacks by 2 against models wholly positioned in Heavy Cover.

#### Vantage Points

1. Vantage Points must be applied to any terrain feature where models can be positioned more than 5" vertically above the ground level of the battlefield.
2. Models making Ranged attacks from a terrain feature with Vantage Points add 1 to their Hit rolls when they are positioned more than 5" vertically above the ground level of the battlefield and are attacking a unit that is positioned at the ground level of the battlefield.

#### Difficult Ground

1. Models that move in to, out of, or through Difficult Ground reduce their Movement characteristic by 1".
2. Difficult Ground has no effect for models with the Fly, Jump Pack, Hover, Phase Shift or Aircraft keywords.

#### Impassable

1. Impassable terrain cannot be moved over or through by any model. This does not apply to models with the Phase Shift or Aircraft keywords
2. The vertical height of the terrain feature should be factored in when moving models that possess keywords that allow them to ignore certain terrain feature vertical heights (i.e., Fly, Hover).

#### Obscuring

1. Any terrain feature that is at least 12" in vertical height relative to the ground level of the battlefield are considered to be Obscuring.
2. Models cannot be the subject of a Ranged attack if the following criteria are met: 
3. The target model is wholly positioned behind Obscuring terrain relative to the attacking model.
4. The attacking model cannot draw true line of sight to the target model without that line of sight passing through Obscuring terrain.

#### Exposed Position

1. Terrain features - or parts of terrain features - that can reasonably be considered as having little or no protective features (like walls, pipes, trees, etc.), and which are at more than 5" tall relative to the ground level of the battlefield should be considered Exposed Positions. 
2. Models making Ranged attacks against models in Exposed Positions increase add 1 to their Hit rolls.

#### Defensible Position

1. Models positioned within a Defensible Position treat models within 2" as being in Base-to-Base contact
2. Models that Charge and Fight models wholly within a terrain feature that has the Defensible Position keyword reduce their Initiative value by 1.

### Terrain Types

The following Terrain Types are a set of standard terrain features you can include on your Alt-Hammer battlefields. Each terrain type is its own keyword - which may itself provide effects for Faction rules or unit abilities - and also shows what other kinds of Terrain Attributes keywords that Terrain Type should have.

#### Hill

1. Hills are considered part of the battlefield's ground level. Other terrain features (such as Buildings) can be placed on Hills if the footprint of that terrain feature can be wholly within the flat surfaces of the Hill feature. 
2. Plain hills that are at least 5" high relative to the ground level of the battlefield, which have discernable obscuring elements (or other Terrain Features on top of the Hill, for example) should possess the **Exposed Position** keyword.

#### Building

1. Many Core Model Type keywords specify how that model can interact with Building terrain features.
2. Buildings always possess the Heavy Cover keyword, and do not feature the Light Cover keyword.
3. Buildings can possess the Defensible Positions keyword, although it is not recommended. Players choosing to incorporate this keyword into the Building should specify areas on the Building considered to be Defensible Positions. 
4. Taller Buildings with 2 or more storeys should have the Obscuring and Vantage Points keywords.

#### Ruins

1. Buildings that have visibly suffered damage should always possess the Ruins keyword.
2. Ruins always possess the Difficult Ground keyword.
3. Taller Ruins with 2 or more storeys should have the Obscuring and Vantage Points keywords.

#### Forest

1. Forests always possess the Light Cover keyword and can never possess the Heavy Cover keyword.
2. Forests should include the Difficult Ground keyword.

#### Craters

1. Craters always include the Difficult Ground keyword.
2. Craters can possess the Light Cover keyword, but can never possess the Heavy Cover keyword.

#### Linear, Low-lying Terrain Features

1. Smaller linear, low-lying terrain features like barricades, fortified lines, pipes, fences etc. possess the Light Cover and Defensible Position keywords.
2. These terrain features should not exceed 2" in height, or 3" in width/depth. Terrain features that exceed those dimensions should be considered a different type of terrain feature.
