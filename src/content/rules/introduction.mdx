Alt-Hammer is a ruleset designed to provide an alternative style of Warhammer 40,000 gameplay using alternating unit activations. 

The intent with Alt-Hammer is to bring Warhammer 40,000's intricate rules designs that are familiar to many Warhammer 40,000 players into more tactical gameplay where each player alternates between individual unit activations, effectively completing their turns - and full battle rounds - simultaneously. 

This document outlines the Core Rules that constitute Alt-Hammer gameplay. It covers:
1. **Preparing Your Game** - the steps required before the game begins, including force organization rules, minimum army requirements, and guidelines for building your army within Alt-Hammer's framework.
2. **Battle Round Structure** - how each of the 3 steps in a battle round are conducted.
3. **Activations & Activation Points** - the core mechanics for unit activations, activation points, and how these govern the interactions between armies during the game.
4. **Attacks - **the process for making attacks, with distinct rules for ranged and melee combat, including how combat is resolved.
5. **Psychic** **Units, Attacks and Abilities** - the mechanics for psychic powers within Alt-Hammer, including how psychic units attempt to activate and resolve such powers.
6. **Battle Shock** - the** **process and effects of Battle Shock, Alt-Hammer's morale system.
7. **Strategic Reserves** - the rules for placing units in Strategic Reserves, including how and when these units can enter the battlefield and any restrictions on their deployment.

### Notes on Alt-Hammer Design Philosophy


Alt-Hammer has been designed with these core philosophies in mind:
1. **Re-rolls are virtually eliminated** - with one exception, there is no rule in Alt-Hammer that provides you the ability to re-roll a D6. And the one exception is optional (it's a Stratagem).
2. **Additive Points** - Alt-Hammer exclusively uses Points to determine battle sizes, and every model and piece of wargear is an additive cost.
3. **High impact attacks against optimal targets** - lethality in Alt-Hammer is optimized for intended targets. Lethality quickly loses efficiency when you make attacks against sub-optimal targets. 
4. **Outside of Faction Rules and Character abilities, virtually all special rules and abilities are universal** - there are very few unit-specific special rules. Once you get familiar with Alt-Hammer's universal Keywords and Abilities, you'll know most of the special rules and abilities in the game; it's just about knowing which rules and abilities are available to a given model or unit, which is done via Keywords.
