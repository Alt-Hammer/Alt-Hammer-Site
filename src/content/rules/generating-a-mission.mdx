The following sections set out a standardized method for generating an Alt-Hammer mission. You can use all of these methods, or a selection within, as best suits how you want to play your game.

## Determine Board Size Deployment Zones {#determine-board-size-deployment-zones}

Alt-Hammer battles are played on a tabletop game board that should align with the following dimensions as best as possible: 
1. 500 Points or Less: 24” by 36”
2. 501 to 1,000 Points: 36” by 48”
3. 1,001 to 2000 Points: 48” by 72” 
4. 2,001 to 3,000 Points: 48” by 96”
5. Above 3,000 Points: Custom to suit your massive game!

## Determine Attacker, Defender, and Deployment Zones {#determine-attacker-defender-and-deployment-zones}

Players should come to a mutually agreeable method of applying these deployment zones to larger Alt-Hammer games if using them is the plan. 

## Deploying Units {#deploying-units}

Once Deployment Zones are identified, players alternate deploying their units onto the battlefield beginning with the Attacker. Player’s can alternate 1 unit at a time, or multiple units at a time at their discretion. 
Players must identify which of their units are either embarked in units with the Transport [X] ability, or placed in Strategic Reserves before placing any units on the battlefield.
Units deploying on the battlefield using the Infiltrators ability must be deployed after all units that deploy normally have been placed. 


## Primary Mission Objectives {#primary-mission-objectives}

Every Alt-Hammer battle played involves securing territory held by your enemy and defending territory you control, which can take the form of Primary Mission Objectives placed on the battlefield. This section explains how to determine your battle’s Primary Mission Objectives.

### Determine Number of Primary Mission Objective Markers {#determine-number-of-primary-mission-objective-markers}

Players each roll a D6. The player with the highest roll then rolls a subsequent D6, the result of which is the total number of Primary Objective Markers that will be used for the battle.

### Place Primary Mission Objective Markers on the Battlefield {#place-primary-mission-objective-markers-on-the-battlefield}

Beginning with the Attacker, players take turns placing the Primary Objective Markers on the battlefield. Objective Markers are placed on the battlefield as follows: 
1. Each player can only place 1 Objective Marker in their own Deployment Zone.
2. An Objective Marker must not be placed within 6” of another Objective Marker.
3. Objective Markers must be placed at least 6” away from any battlefield edge.
4. Objective Markers in No Man’s Land cannot be placed within 6” of a Deployment Zone’s boundaries.

Successfully controlling Primary Objective Markers earns players a number of Victory Points as defined in the table below. These Victory Points are tallied during Step 5 of the Command Phase.

#### Deployment Zone Objective Markers {#deployment-zone-objective-markers}

1. Objective markers positioned in the deployment zone of the player who controls that objective marker reduce their Victory Points value by 2. 
2. Objective markers positioned in a deployment zone that are controlled by the opposing player (i.e., the Attacker controlling an objective marker in the Defender’s deployment zone) increase their Victory Points value by 2.

## Optional Game Feature: Secondary Mission Objectives {#optional-game-feature-secondary-mission-objectives}

Players can utilize Secondary Mission Objectives as an optional way to enhance gameplay for Alt-Hammer battles. To do so, at the start of each Battle Round, each player rolls a D12 (or 2D6) 3 times. The resulting rolls indicate which of the following Secondary Mission Objectives will be in effect for each player’s army for the duration of the Battle Round. 
Secondary Mission Objectives that are not completed by the end of the Battle Round can be retained for the next Battle Round, but completed Secondary Mission Objectives must be replaced at that start of the subsequent Battle Round. Each time a player generates a Secondary Mission Objective that is impossible to complete by the end of the battle round, the player can discard the Secondary Mission Objective and regenerate another to replace it.
