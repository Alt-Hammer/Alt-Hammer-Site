The following sections set out a standardized method for generating an Alt-Hammer battle. You can use all of these methods, or a selection within, as best suits how you want to play your game.


## Determine Board Size Deployment Zones


Alt-Hammer battles are played on a tabletop game board that should align with the following dimensions as best as possible: 
- 500 Points or Less: 24" by 36"
- 501 to 1,000 Points: 36" by 48"
- 1,001 to 2000 Points: 48" by 72" 
- 2,001 to 3,000 Points: 48" by 96"
- Above 3,000 Points: Custom to suit your massive game!

## Determine Attacker, Defender, and Deployment Zones


With a game size and board size established, players must determine who will be the Attacker and Defender, and then determine which Deployment Zone will be used for the battle. 

To determine Attacker and Defender, each player rolls a D6. The player with the highest roll is the Attacker.

The Attacker then rolls a D6 to select which Deployment Zone will be used for the battle, the result of which corresponds to 1 of the following Deployment Zones:

It should be noted that these deployment zones are designed with a standard 2,000 points or less game and 2:3 game board ratio in mind, wherein a gap equal to one third of the total board length - being 24" on that 2,000-point game board size - equals the minimum distance between deployment zones along any frontage. Players should come to a mutually agreeable method of applying these deployment zones to larger Alt-Hammer games if using them is the plan. 


## Deploying Units


Once Deployment Zones are identified, players alternate deploying their units onto the battlefield beginning with the Attacker. Player's can alternate 1 unit at a time, or multiple units at a time at their discretion. 

Players must identify which of their units are either embarked in units with the <span class="keyword" data-term="transport-x" data-type="keyword">Transport [X] </span>ability, or placed in Strategic Reserves before placing any units on the battlefield.

Units deploying on the battlefield using the <span class="keyword" data-term="infiltrators" data-type="keyword">Infiltrators</span> ability must be deployed after all units that deploy normally have been placed. 


## Primary Mission Objectives


Every Alt-Hammer battle played involves securing territory held by your enemy and defending territory you control, which can take the form of Primary Mission Objectives placed on the battlefield. This section explains how to determine your battle's Primary Mission Objectives.


### Determine Number of Primary Mission Objective Markers


Players each roll a D6. The player with the highest roll then rolls a subsequent D6, the result of which is the total number of Primary Objective Markers that will be used for the battle.


### Place Primary Mission Objective Markers on the Battlefield


Beginning with the Attacker, players take turns placing the Primary Objective Markers on the battlefield. Objective Markers are placed on the battlefield as follows: 
- There must always be at least 1 Objective Marker that is positioned in No Man's Land at least 9" away from the boundaries of every player's Deployment Zone.
- Each player can only place 1 Objective Marker in their own Deployment Zone.
- An Objective Marker must not be placed within 6" of another Objective Marker.
- Objective Markers must be placed at least 6" away from any battlefield edge.
- Objective Markers in No Man's Land cannot be placed within 6" of a Deployment Zone's boundaries.

### Holding Objectives


Securing and controlling objectives is the heart of victory in Alt-Hammer 40,000. The following table outlines the Objective Control capabilities for various model types. 

| Model Traits Used to Determine Objective Control | Objective Control Value |
| --- | --- |
| Infantry, Beasts and Swarms models with Toughness from 1 to 2 | 1 |
| Infantry, Beasts and Swarms models with Toughness 3 | 2 |
| Infantry, Beasts and Swarms models with Toughness 4 | 3 |
| Infantry, Beasts and Swarms models with Toughness from 5 to 6 | 4 |
| Infantry, Beasts and Swarms models with Toughness from 7 to 10 | 5 |
| Monsters & Vehicles models with Toughness up to 11 | # of Remaining Wounds |
| Monsters & Vehicles models with Toughness 12 and above | # of Remaining Wounds x2 |
| Any of the above with the Character keyword | +3 |
| Any of the above with the Epic Hero keyword | Any of the above x2 |
| Models with the Aircraft keyword | 0 |

To determine who controls a Primary Objective Marker, each player tallies up to the total Objective Control value for their models wholly within 3" of a Primary Objective Marker. The player with the highest Objective Control value controls that objective marker. 


### Victory Points for Controlling Objectives


Successfully controlling Primary Objective Markers earns players a number of Victory Points as defined in the table below. These Victory Points are tallied during Step 5 of the Command Phase.

| Number of Primary Objective Markers Currently on the Battlefield | Victory Points per Primary Objective Marker |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | 24 |
| 2 | 12 |
| 3 | 8 |
| 4 | 6 |
| 5 | 5 |
| 6 | 4 |


<h6 id="deployment-zone-objective-markers">Deployment Zone Objective Markers</h6>

- Objective markers positioned in the deployment zone of the player who controls that objective marker reduce their Victory Points value by 2. 
- Objective markers positioned in a deployment zone that are controlled by the opposing player (i.e., the Attacker controlling an objective marker in the Defender's deployment zone) increase their Victory Points value by 2.

## Optional Game Feature: Determine Mission Rule


Players can utilize Mission Rules as an optional way to enhance gameplay for Alt-Hammer battles. To do so, one player rolls a D6. The resulting roll indicates which of the following Mission Rules will be in effect for the duration of the battle.

| # | Name | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Treacherous Conditions | Models reduce their Movement characteristic by 1/3, rounding down. Models with the Aircraft, Fly, Jump Pack or Hover keywords are unaffected by this Mission Rule. |
| 2 | Adaptive Camouflage | Units with the Stealth or Small Profile keyword gain an additional -1 to be Hit by Ranged attacks if they are in Cover. |
| 3 | Overcharged Assault | Weapons with the Assault or Pistol keyword gain +3" to their Range characteristic this Battle Round. |
| 4 | Saboteur's Opportunity | Units completing a Mission Task action within 3" of a Primary Objective Marker may roll a D6; on a 4+, the Objective Marker is considered "Sabotaged" and cannot be controlled by the enemy until the end of the next Battle Round. |
| 5 | Data Uplink | If a unit completes a Mission Task action while wholly within 6" of a Primary Objective Marker, immediately gain 1 additional Command Point. |
| 6 | Fortified Position | At the end of the Battle Round, if a unit Remained Stationary on a Primary Objective Marker for the entire round, that Objective Marker grants +1 Objective Control to all friendly units with models on the objective until control of the objective is lost to the opposing player. |


## Optional Game Feature: Secondary Mission Objectives

The resulting rolls indicate which of the following Secondary Mission Objectives will be in effect for each player's army for the duration of the Battle Round. 

Secondary Mission Objectives that are not completed by the end of the Battle Round can be retained for the next Battle Round, but completed Secondary Mission Objectives must be replaced at that start of the subsequent Battle Round. Each time a player generates a Secondary Mission Objective that is impossible to complete by the end of the Battle Round - a fact which should be agreed-to by all players - the player can discard the Secondary Mission Objective and regenerate another to replace it.
