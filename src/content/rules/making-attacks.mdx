Your units and models can make attacks against enemy units and models both at Range by using guns, psychic powers, grenades, etc., and up close in Melee by using melee weapons, or even just their own physical might. General rules for each of these kinds of attacks are described here, with additional rules unique to Ranged and Melee attacks detailed afterwards.


## General Sequence of Operations for Making Attacks

1. **Roll to Hit** after reviewing the Hit Roll Table to determine the value required to achieve a successful Hit - which is established using a comparison between the attacking model and target model's Weapons Skill or Ballistic Skill characteristics - and applying any applicable modifiers to the relevant characteristic or Hit roll from abilities that may be affecting this attack.
2. **Roll to Wound** by comparing the attack's Strength characteristic to the target unit's Toughness characteristic, using the applicable dice roll value found on the Wound Roll Table.
3. **Successfully wounded units or models must then make an Armour Save** against that attack to determine whether that Wound successfully inflicts the value of that attack's Damage characteristic to them. This is done after accounting for any abilities that are triggered by a Critical Wound (ie, <span class="keyword" data-term="devastating-wounds" data-type="keyword">Devastating Wounds</span>). Remember to account for any Armour Piercing negative modifiers to the target's Armour Save as well as any defensive abilities (ie, Terrain features, <span class="keyword" data-term="invulnerable-save-x" data-type="keyword">Invulnerable Save [X]+</span>) that may impact this roll. 
4. **If wounded successfully, apply the applicable Damage to the target unit or model** as determined by the attacking weapon's Damage characteristic. Account for any abilities that can reduce Damage (ie, <span class="keyword" data-term="feel-no-pain-x" data-type="keyword">Feel No Pain [X]+</span>) that the target unit or model may have. Damage from multiple attacks that successfully Wound can be applied across units with multiple models in them at the defending player's discretion. However, each individual attack must have all of its damage allocated to a single model. 

### General Conditions Applicable to Making Attacks

1. Models cannot select enemy units or models as the target of an attack if that model is incapable of successfully hitting that target (because the attacking model's Weapons Skill or Ballistic Skill is too low relative to the target model/unit). Check the Hit Roll Table to confirm and remember to account for any applicable modifiers to confirm that your attacking model is able to Hit. If it's not able to Hit, choose another target, or, if none are available, the model is not eligible to make those attacks.
2. Models cannot select enemy units or models as the target of an attack if that model is incapable of successfully Wounding that target for any reason; ie, the attack's Strength characteristic would be too low to Wound the model. The Wound Rolls subsection includes a Wound Table that displays the dice roll values needed to successfully Wound.
3. Attacks that have a Strength characteristic that is more than three times the value of the target's toughness characteristic automatically destroy that target upon a successful Hit roll. The target model or unit cannot use any saving throws of any kind, and one model is destroyed for each attack that meets these conditions.
4. Hit Rolls are made using a model's Ballistic Skill for Ranged attacks, and their Weapon Skill for Melee attacks. Hit rolls of 1 always fail, and rolls of 6 always count as a Critical Hit. Be sure to account for any abilities that may alter Hit rolls, such as abilities that enable Critical Hits to be scored on a 5 instead of a 6. However, if a model is not able to Hit a target per the Hit Roll Table, then that model is not able to score a Critical Hit on that target.
5. Wound Rolls of 1 always fail, and rolls of 6 always count as a Critical Wound. Be sure to account for any abilities that may alter Wound rolls, such as abilities that enable Critical Wounds to be scored on a 5 instead of a 6. However, if a model is not able to Wound a target per the Wound Roll Table, then that model is not able to score a Critical Wound.
6. Hit rolls and Wound rolls can be subject to negative and positive modifiers attributed to abilities or special rules applicable to that attack. The cumulative effect of these modifiers cannot exceed a total change of +/- 1 to that dice roll. 

### Armour Saves


Every model in Alt-Hammer possesses an Armour Save characteristic. These values are always between 2+ and 7+. A roll equal to or greater than the value shown in the Armour Save characteristic - made on 1 D6 - means the model avoids being Wounded and having Damage inflicted on it. This is referred to as a successful Saving Throw. A roll lower than the value shown in the Armour Save characteristic is a failed Saving Throw, resulting in the model being successfully Wounded and having Damage inflicted on it.

Armour Save characteristics are affected by other rules and abilities - most notably Armour Piercing characteristics of weapons, <span class="keyword" data-term="light-cover" data-type="keyword">Light Cover</span>, and <span class="keyword" data-term="heavy-cover" data-type="keyword">Heavy Cover</span>, and the <span class="keyword" data-term="invulnerable-save-x" data-type="keyword">Invulnerable Save [X]+</span> ability, although other unique abilities may also impact Saving Throws as well. These rules and abilities can decrease, or increase, to the value needed for a successful Saving Throw. In any instance, a roll of a 1 is always considered to be a failed Saving Throw, and as such, any modifications that improve an Armour Save characteristic beyond 2+ (i.e., benefitting from <span class="keyword" data-term="heavy-cover" data-type="keyword">Heavy Cover</span>) are ignored. Any Armour Save characteristic exceeding 6+ - from, for example, models with low standard Armour Save characteristics suffering Wounds from weapons with high Armour Piercing values - are always considered to be automatically failed, with no Saving Throw required to be made. 


### Mortal Wounds


Several abilities common to model and wargear keywords cause Mortal Wounds to be inflicted. Models that suffer Mortal Wounds cannot take Saving Throws against these wounds using their Armour Save characteristic or with the <span class="keyword" data-term="invulnerable-save-x" data-type="keyword">Invulnerable Save [X]+</span> ability. However, each point of Damage inflicted through Mortal Wounds can be avoided with the <span class="keyword" data-term="feel-no-pain-x" data-type="keyword">Feel No Pain [X]+</span> ability.


### Hit Roll Table


Successful Hit rolls are determined by comparing the attacking model's Weapons or Ballistics Skill characteristic against the target unit's lowest applicable Weapons or Ballistics Skill characteristic. The appropriate values for these comparisons are established using the Hit Roll Table below, noting that:
1. Values of **auto** mean that target units or models are automatically Hit, and that the Hit is considered a Critical Hit. 
2. Values of **x** mean that the attacker's Weapons or Ballistics Skill characteristic is too low to effectively Hit the target model or unit, and thus those attacks cannot be made against that target unit or model; the target unit is simply far too skilled at dodging shots or parrying blows!

Hit rolls are often subject to positive or negative modifiers caused by attacking model abilities or target model abilities, however, those roll modifiers cannot supersede the limitations set forth by auto and x values. For example, if a model with a Weapons Skill of 2 is attempting to Hit a model with a Weapons Skill of 7 and is benefitting from a rule that gives them +1 to their Hit roll, their attack will still fail because they have insufficient Weapons Skill to attack the target effectively. However, if that same model was instead benefitting from an ability that increased its Weapons Skill characteristic by 1 (bringing it to a Weapons Skill 3), the model would then be able to Hit the target on a 6+.


### Wound Roll Table

Wound rolls may be subject to positive or negative modifiers caused by attacking model abilities or target model abilities, however, those roll modifiers cannot supersede the limitations set forth by **auto** and x values.

### "Hit Them from Behind!"


Models with the <span class="keyword" data-term="vehicle" data-type="keyword">Vehicle</span> or <span class="keyword" data-term="monster" data-type="keyword">Monster</span> keyword reduce their Toughness characteristic by 1 against attacks that can draw true, unobstructed line of sight to the rear quadrant of the model, and only when that quadrant is the closest part of the model to the attacking model.

For <span class="keyword" data-term="vehicles" data-type="keyword">Vehicles</span>, the rear quadrant of the model is considered the full back face of its hull relative to the primary direction that the vehicle moves forward. In the example below, the Rear quadrant is highlighted.

## Shooting Attacks


The <span class="keyword" data-term="shoot" data-type="action">Shoot</span> Action involves the activated unit making a Ranged attack with one or more of its Ranged Weapons, and do so by following these steps:
1. Select which Ranged weapon(s) your unit or model will make an attack with. The number of weapons a model can make Ranged attacks with is determined by its Model Characteristic Keywords found on its data profile. 
2. Allocate your Ranged attacks to a target enemy unit, or units. Each attack can target a different unit of your choosing, but one individual Ranged weapon cannot target more than one enemy unit, unless that Weapon has keywords that impact how its attacks are allocated to targets (ie, <span class="keyword" data-term="blast-x" data-type="keyword">Blast [X]"</span>).
3. Determine if your attacking model has line of sight. Alt-Hammer uses True Line of Sight, which involves the player getting as close as possible to the attacking model's physical line of sight on the tabletop to determine what it can or cannot see. A model has line of sight when it can see any part of at least one model in a target enemy unit. 
4. If your attacking model cannot draw line of sight to at least 1 model in the target enemy unit then it cannot make a Ranged attack at that unit, and you must choose a different target unit if possible. Please note that the <span class="keyword" data-term="indirect-fire" data-type="keyword">Indirect Fire</span> ability supersedes this limitation.
5. Models that are less than 50% visible to the attacking enemy model - but which are not positioned wholly within any terrain feature - gain <span class="keyword" data-term="light-cover" data-type="keyword">Light Cover</span>, as further detailed in the Terrain Attributes section of these rules. 
6. If the model is on a base, the base should be used to determine whether less than 50% of the model is visible.
7. Complete the Attacks sequence of operations as detailed prior.


## Melee Attacks


The <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> and <span class="keyword" data-term="fight" data-type="action">Fight</span> Actions allow eligible units from each army to make Melee attacks with their Melee weapons. A unit is considered engaged in these Actions - and thus eligible to make Melee attacks - if at least 1 of its models is in Base-to-Base contact with at least 1 enemy model.

Before resolving Melee attacks, determine the attack sequence by comparing Initiative characteristics for all involved models. Models with the highest Initiative attack first, moving down through those with lower Initiative. Remember: models that completed a <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> Action gain +1 to their Initiative for this sequence. 

Fights are made by following these steps:
1. **Pile In move: **Before making their Melee attacks, models that are not currently in Base-to-Base contact complete a Pile In move up to a distance equal to their Initiative characteristic. This Pile In move must end either with the model in base-to-base contact with an enemy model, base-to-base contact with a friendly model in base-to-base contact with an enemy model, or as close as possible to either of those positions. Pile In moves cannot be made over any other model's base, and unit coherency must be maintained at the end of a Pile In move.
2. **Melee attack eligibility: **Models that are eligible to make a Melee attack must either be in base-to-base contact with an enemy model, or in base-to-base contact with a friendly model that is in base-to-base contact with an enemy model. 
3. **Select Melee Weapon: **Models can allocate the number of attacks they have as determined by their Attacks Characteristic to 1 Melee weapon they are equipped with (using that weapon's profile to make those attacks), as well as make Melee attacks with any weapons they are equipped with that have the <span class="keyword" data-term="extra-attacks" data-type="keyword">Extra Attacks</span> ability. 
4. If a model is not equipped with a Melee weapon of any kind, it can still make attacks using the Attacks and Strength characteristics on its model profile; these attacks always have an Armour Piercing value of 0 and Damage characteristic of 1, and typically never have any abilities conferred by Keywords (however, some factions may receive Faction Rules or Detachment abilities that impact these attacks). 
5. **Complete Melee attacks: **Models complete their Melee attacks following the general attack order of operations detailed prior. 
6. **Impacts of Initiative: **The results of Melee attacks from models with higher Initiative values will impact whether models with lower Initiative values will have the chance to make their own Melee attacks. For example, a model with an Initiative value of 5 may kill several models with Initiative values of 3 before they even get a chance to strike! Units with the same Initiative value are considered to be making their Melee attacks at the exact same time, and thus would get to make their attacks regardless of the sequence the players complete the dice rolls for those attacks.
7. **Removing Casualties:** Casualties from Melee attacks must first be removed from models in base-to-base contact with the model(s) making those melee attacks before removing casualties from models positioned outside of base-to-base contact.
8. This order of casualty removal can be ignored in instances where a <span class="keyword" data-term="character" data-type="keyword">Character</span> model would need to be removed as a casualty before a <span class="keyword" data-term="bodyguard" data-type="keyword">Bodyguard</span> model. In such instances it is considered that the <span class="keyword" data-term="bodyguard" data-type="keyword">Bodyguard</span> models would be doing everything possible to put themselves in harms way before letting their Leader fall!
9. **Consolidation move: **If a Fight sequence ends and there are no models left in base-to-base contact with the attacking model(s), then they can make a Consolidation move of up to 3" if you choose to do so. Consolidation moves must be made towards either the closest enemy unit, or the closest Primary Objective Marker. Unit coherency must be maintained at the end of this Consolidation move, and the unit is no longer be eligible to make Melee attacks as part of the initial <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> or <span class="keyword" data-term="fight" data-type="action">Fight</span> Action.

### Engaging Additional Units Over the Course of a Fight


Additional units can become eligible to make Melee attacks as part of the initial <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> or <span class="keyword" data-term="fight" data-type="action">Fight</span> Action should the completion of Pile In or Consolidation moves result new units having models in Base-to-Base Contact with models from units that were already completing Melee attacks. Be sure to consider the Initiative characteristic of these newly engaged units when completing subsequent Melee attacks.


### Multiple Melee Attacks within a Battle Round


A unit can be triggered to complete Melee attacks a part of a <span class="keyword" data-term="fight" data-type="action">Fight</span> Action any number of times in a single Battle Round. However, each successive time that that same unit makes Melee attacks it will suffer some degradation of its Melee capabilities through reductions in select model characteristics, which remain in effect until the end of the Battle Round. This is to represent models in the unit gradually being worn down by the repeated onslaught of enemies without having a chance to regain their composure and refocus. The following scale degradation steps are used for this purpose:
1. 1st Melee attack - no negative effects are applied
2. 2nd Melee attack onwards - reduce Attacks characteristic by 1 for each successive Melee attack, to a minimum of 1.
3. 3rd Melee attack onwards - reduce Initiative characteristic by 1 for each successive Melee attack, to a minimum of 1.
4. 4th Melee attack onwards - reduce Weapons Skill characteristic by 1 for each successive Melee attack, to a minimum of 1.
5. 5th Melee attack onwards - the unit's Weapons Skill, Attacks and Initiative continue to be reduced, and the unit is automatically Battle Shocked after completing their 5th Melee attack.

#### Example Scenario


Player One has a unit that has been triggered to complete Melee attacks 3 times during the Battle Round. Models in that unit originally had Weapons Skill of 5, an Initiative of 4, and 3 Attacks. However, after multiple melee engagements in short succession, the surviving models in the unit have been beaten down and are exhausted, but are still fighting as best as they can to survive. The unit has once again been triggered to make Melee attacks resulting from yet another enemy unit from Player Two targeting them as part of a <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> Action. Since this will be the 4th <span class="keyword" data-term="fight" data-type="action">Fight</span> Action the unit is forced to participate in this Battle Round, the characteristics of models in the unit are modified as follows: 
1. Weapons Skill of 4 (-1 from this latest 4th Melee)
2. Initiative of 2 (-1 from 3rd Melee, -1 from this latest 4th Melee)
3. 1 Attack (-1 from 2nd Melee, -1 from 3rrd Melee, -1 from this latest 4th Melee, now at the minimum of 1 and cannot be reduced further) 

Should this unit miraculously survive this latest Melee engagement and have the misfortune of being the target of *yet another* enemy unit as part of a <span class="keyword" data-term="charge-and-fight" data-type="action">Charge and Fight</span> Action, the models in that unit's relevant characteristics would be modified as follows:
1. Weapons Skill of 3 (-1 from this latest 5th Melee plus prior reduction from 4th Melee)
2. Initiative of 1 (-1 from 3rd Melee, -1 from 4th Melee, -1 from this latest 5th Melee, now at the minimum of 1 and cannot be reduced further)
3. 1 Attacks (reduced to the minimum of 1 from the prior 4th Melee)

Should models in the unit survive this 5th Fight as well and have even more misfortune to endure a 5th Fight, their Weapons Skill would be reduced to 2, Initiative and Attacks would remain at 1 (the lowest they can go), and the unit would become Battle Shocked once their Melee attacks are completed.
