Units can become Battle Shocked when they suffer damage or casualties that result in the unit being below Half Strength, or are the subject of an enemy unit ability that forces a Battle Shock Test. A unit is at Half Strength when it has less than half of the unit’s total Wounds it started the Battle Round with.

Units that must take a Battle Shock Test do so immediately upon being at or lower than Half Strength, or when an enemy unit forces a Battle Shock Test. This can occur at any time during the Activation Phase of the turn. Complete a Battle Shock Test as follows: 
1. Roll 2 D6.
2. The unit successfully passes the Battle Shock Test if the result of that 2 D6 roll is equal to or greater than the unit’s Leadership characteristic, resulting in no Battle Shock effects occurring. 
3. The unit fails the Battle Shock Test if the result of that 2 D6 roll is lower than the unit’s Leadership Characteristic. The unit is then considered to be Battle Shocked.
4. A unit’s Leadership characteristic can be modified if any of the following apply for the Battle Shock Tests:
5. Reduce Leadership characteristic by 1 for each enemy unit that has models in Base-to-Base contact with models in the unit. Further reduce Leadership by 1 for each of these enemy units that possesses the <span class="keyword" data-term="monster" data-type="keyword">Monster</span> or <span class="keyword" data-term="daemon" data-type="keyword">Daemon</span> keywords.
6. Reduce Leadership characteristic by 1 for each <span class="keyword" data-term="titanic" data-type="keyword">Titanic</span> unit within 6”
7. Increase Leadership characteristic by 1 for each friendly unit that has at least 1 model within 6” of the unit.
8. Units that are Battle Shocked are subject to the following until the unit successfully passes a Rally Check:
9. Reduce their Activation Points to 1. 
10. Can only use the <span class="keyword" data-term="fall-back" data-type="action">Fall Back</span>, <span class="keyword" data-term="shoot" data-type="action">Shoot</span> and <span class="keyword" data-term="fight" data-type="action">Fight</span> Actions. 
11. Hit rolls for Ranged and Melee attacks are reduced by 1
12. If the unit has the <span class="keyword" data-term="battleline" data-type="keyword">Battleline</span> keyword, that unit is no longer eligible to make Bonus Activations for the remainder of the Battle Round.

#### Design Note


Remember to apply any modifications to the unit’s Leadership characteristic that may be in effect when taking your Battle Shock Test! These could be negative or positive modifications conferred by things like unit abilities, Leader units, enemy Faction abilities that reduce Leadership characteristics, etc.


### Rally Checks


At the start of the subsequent Battle Round’s Command Phase, each player completes a Rally Check for any units that are currently Battle Shocked. A Rally Check is completed by rolling 2 D6, with the resulting effects as follows:
1. If the roll is lower than the unit’s Leadership characteristic, the unit does not Rally and remains Battle Shocked for the remainder of the Battle Round.
2. If the roll is higher than the unit’s Leadership characteristic, the unit Rallies and is no longer Battle Shocked. A unit’s Leadership characteristic is subject to the same modifiers as the Battle Shock Test, should any be applicable.
