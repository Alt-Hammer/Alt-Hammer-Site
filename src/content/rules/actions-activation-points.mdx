Every unit in your army possesses 3 Activation Points, although this number can be modified through unit or faction abilities. Activation Points are used by a unit when it is Activated to complete any of the Actions described in this section. Some limitations on Actions usage eligibility and sequencing – ie, which Actions can be used when, and under what conditions – are also identified. Some units in your army may be able to use Bonus Activations, the rules for which are described in this section.

## Core Actions {#core-actions}


#### Remain Stationary	2 Activation Points {#remain-stationary-2-activation-points}

This Action can only be used by a unit as its first Action. The selected unit entrenches itself in a secure, defensible position. No other Actions aside from Shoot can be used as part the unit’s Activation thereafter. A unit that chose to Remain Stationary increases its Initiative characteristic by 1 until the end of the Battle Round. 

#### Move	1 Activation Point {#move-1-activation-point}

The selected unit may Move up to the maximum Movement characteristic detailed on its datasheet, and in accordance with any Model Characteristic Keywords impacting how that unit can move. 
This Action cannot be used under the following conditions:
1. Your unit is within Base-to-base contact of any enemy models.
2. Your unit has already used this Action once as part of its activation. 

Units can choose to Advance as part of a Move Action. An Advance is additional movement completed as follows:
1. The unit completes its Advance movement up to a distance in inches equal to the lowest Initiative value in that unit.
2. Units that Advance are not eligible to Shoot with any weapons other than weapons that possess the Pistol or Assault keyword.
3. Units that Advance are not eligible to Charge and Fight, unless they possess an ability or model keyword that enables them to do so.

#### Fall Back 	1 Activation Point {#fall-back-1-activation-point}

The selected unit may Fall Back as its first Action if it begins its Activation within Base-to-base contact of enemy models. It may Fall Back up to the maximum Movement characteristic detailed on its datasheet, and must not end this Fall Back move closer to any enemy models than when it began. Units that Fall Back cannot then use remaining Activation Points for any of the following actions: Move*, *Shoot*, *and Charge and Fight* *unless that unit has access to abilities, or Stratagems that allow it to complete other Actions after making a Fall Back Action.

#### Enter the Battlefield from Strategic Reserve 	2 Activation Points {#enter-the-battlefield-from-strategic-reserve-2-activation-points}

Units held in Strategic Reserves may enter the battlefield as their first Action. Units that do so may not then use Move.


#### Embark or Disembark from a TRANSPORT	1 / 2 Activation Points {#embark-or-disembark-from-a-transport-1-2-activation-points}

This Action is utilized by the selected unit in one of several ways:
1. **Disembark: **If the selected unit is currently embarked in a Transport, it can use this Action to Disembark from that Transport unit. Models that disembark must first be placed within 3” of the Transport unit before spending any more Activation Points. 
2. Units that Disembark from a Transport that has not yet been activated this turn may then use their remaining Activation Points to complete any of the following eligible Actions: Move*, *Shoot*, *Charge and Fight, adhering to any limitations to the sequencing of those activations where applicable. Disembarking under this condition costs 1 Activation Point.
3. Units that Disembark from a Transport that has been activated this turn may not use the Charge and Fight Action, but can otherwise act normally. Disembarking under this condition costs 2 Activation Points.
4. **Embark: **If all models in the selected unit are within 3” of a friendly Transport, it may use this Action to embark into that Transport. It may then no longer use any remaining Activation Points, unless that Transport’s datasheet possesses unique abilities that allow units to function while embarked (ie, making Ranged attacks via the Firing Deck [X] ability). Embarking always costs 1 Activation Point.
5. **Emergency Disembarkation**: When a Transport model is destroyed, every unit embarked within it at that time must immediately make an Emergency Disembarkation. To do so, Roll 1 D6 for each model in the unit. For each roll of a 1 – or a roll of a 1 or 2 if the Transport being destroyed triggered the Deadly Demise [X] ability – the unit suffers 1 Mortal Wound. Models not destroyed at this stage are then placed within 3” of the Transport model. The unit then takes a Battle Shock test, or is automatically considered to be Battle Shocked if the Transport being destroyed triggered the Deadly Demise [X] ability.

Restrictions detailed here can be overruled if the Activated unit has abilities that affect interactions with Transport units. For example, some units (including Transports) may provide the ability to allow embarked units to be eligible to Charge and Fight after disembarking from a Transport that has Moved this turn.

#### Shoot	1 Activation Point {#shoot-1-activation-point}

The selected unit may make Ranged attacks with all models in the unit eligible to do so. Units that Shoot* *may then spend remaining Activation Points on any other Actions, but must follow any Action restrictions affecting that unit caused by Wargear abilities (ie, Heavy, Ordnance).

#### Charge and Fight	1 Activation Point {#charge-and-fight-1-activation-point}

The selected unit may complete this Action if they have already completed a Move Action or used the Enter the Battlefield from Strategic Reserves Action. This Action is completed as follows: 
1. The selected unit Charges an enemy unit (or units) by moving a charge move distance in Inches equal to D6 + the lowest Initiative characteristic in the unit. At least 1 model from the unit must be able to get into Base-to-Base Contact with a model from the enemy unit being Charged in order for the Charge to be considered successful.
2. Resolve Melee attacks using the Fight Order of Operations detailed under the Making Attacks section of the Core Rules. The unit that completed this Charge and Fight Action increases their Initiative value by 1 for this Fight.

If the selected unit has any remaining Activation Points after completing the Charge and Fight Activation, and there are no longer any enemy models within Base-to-Base contact with any models in the unit, it may then continue to spend them on the following eligible Actions:* *Move,* *Shoot*, *Embark or Disembark from a TRANSPORT*.*

#### Fight	1 Activation Point {#fight-1-activation-point}

The selected unit may Fight, following the order of operations described under the Melee Attacks rules section, under 1 of the 2 following scenarios: 
1. As part of the unit’s own Activation if it has models is within Base-to-base contact with enemy models when it is Activated, using its first Activation Point. Units selected to use the Fight* *Action in this manner may then spend any remaining Activation Points on other eligible Actions, excluding the following: Embark or Disembark from a TRANSPORT, Enter the Battlefield from Strategic Reserve, and Charge and Fight.
2. When it is the target of an enemy unit’s Charge and Fight* *Action, wherein this Action does not cost the unit an Activation Point because it is must complete this Action as part of that enemy unit’s Charge and Fight Action.

#### Ascend to the Skies	2 Activation Points {#ascend-to-the-skies-2-activation-points}

This Action can only be used by units with at least one of the following keywords: Aircraft, Fly, or Jump Pack. Remove the unit from the table and place it into Strategic Reserves.

#### Mission Task	2 Activation Points {#mission-task-2-activation-points}

This Action allows the unit to complete any Mission Task as determined by an active Primary or Secondary Objective.
Units cannot select this Action if any models in the unit are in Base-to-Base contact with any enemy models. Further this Action can only be used by a unit if that unit did not use any of the following Actions as part of this Activation: Shoot*, *Charge and Fight*, *Fight*, *Fall Back*. *Units that used this Action cannot be selected for a Bonus Activation during the current Battle Round.

#### Hold Fast	All Activation Points {#hold-fast-all-activation-points}

This Action allows the unit to effectively ‘pass’ on being Activated this Battle Round. The unit must declare it will Hold Fast at the start of its Activation, and cannot use any subsequent Actions under any conditions whatsoever. This Action cannot be used if the unit is: 
1. In Base-to-Base Contact with any enemy units, 
2. or
3. Has any models within 6” of any enemy units.

Units that Hold Fast are not eligible to complete a Bonus Activation this Battle Round. 

## Bonus Activations {#bonus-activations}

If a player finishes activating all of their units before the other player, they may select previously Activated Battleline units again for a Bonus Activation.
A Bonus Activation grants the selected unit 1 Activation Point, which can be used for 1 of the following Actions if that Action’s eligibility criteria and limitations are met:

A unit can be selected to complete a Bonus Activation once, and each Bonus Activation can only be used once per Battle Round. In other words, a player can only be entitled to a maximum of 4 Bonus Activations in total; 1 Shoot Action, 1 Move Action, 1 Charge and Fight Action, and 1 Fight* *Action. These Bonus Activations can be chosen in any order.
If all units in an army have been Activated, and then each unit eligible to complete a Bonus Activation have also done so, the player may no longer complete any Activations this Battle Round and the opposing player must complete any outstanding unit Activations they have in the order they so choose.

#### Example Scenario {#example-scenario}

Player One has 10 units in their army and Player Two has 8 units in their army, including 1 unit with the Battleline keyword. 
Player Two has the Initiative this Battle Round, and completes each of their 8 units’ activations over the course of the Activation Phase (which begins with one of their units). By the time Player Two is finished all of their unit activations, Player One still has 2 units left to activate. This means that Player Two will be able to complete 1 Bonus Activations: they have 1 Battleline unit in their army, and Player One has two remaining units to activate. Player Two’s first Bonus Activation will occur after Player One completes their 8th unit activation (this will effectively be Player Two’s 9th activation), after which Player One will complete their 9th unit activation. Player Two’s first Bonus Activation was completed with their army’s one and only Battleline unit, and therefore they do not have any other units that are eligible for Bonus Activations. As such, Player One will complete their 10th and final unit activation immediately after their 9th unit activation, after which the Activation Phase for this Battle Round is completed.

#### Design Note {#design-note}

Bonus Activations are intended to give armies with lower unit counts a modest degree of support and flexibility when fighting against armies with a significantly larger number of units (which itself may be a tactical advantage in Alt-Hammer!). Bonus Activations are only eligible to be used by units with the Battleline keyword (ie, standard rank-and-file troops for most factions). This limitation is designed to discourage list-building strategies that seek to leverage Bonus Activations to double down on utility and damage output from high value, specialized units.
