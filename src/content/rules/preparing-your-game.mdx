## Faction Rules


Every Alt-hammer Faction falls under one of the following overarching Factions: 
1. <span class="keyword" data-term="imperium" data-type="keyword">Imperium</span>
2. <span class="keyword" data-term="chaos" data-type="keyword">Chaos</span>
3. <span class="keyword" data-term="aeldari" data-type="keyword">Aeldari</span>
4. <span class="keyword" data-term="the-great-devourer" data-type="keyword">The Great Devourer</span>
5. <span class="keyword" data-term="broader-xenos-species" data-type="keyword">Broader Xenos Species</span>. 

Each Faction within these groups offers:
1. **Faction Rules:** one or more unique abilities units that units from that Faction benefit from when your Army is built with that Faction. You will note that many Faction Rules allow a Faction to be taken as an additional Faction if your Warlord is from another allied Faction, or allow you to include units from multiple allied Factions in some limited capacity.
2. **Detachment Traits:** an additional list of traits that allow you to customize your Detachment thematically, offering play style and detachment force composition flexibility within a Faction's available units. Detachment Traits are sometimes selected using Detachment Points, and limitations on which Detachment Traits can be taken alongside others are prevalent within each Faction's list of Detachment Traits.
3. **Wargear Upgrades:** Many units and models in each Faction have access to various forms of Wargear that they can be equipped with, some unique and others less so. The points cost and rules for these upgrades are detailed for each Faction under a Wargear Upgrades section. 

## Force Organization


The Faction you play a game of Alt-Hammer with is comprised of Units. There is no limit on the total number of Units your force can include, however, your army's force organization must abide by the following limitations applied to units that feature specific Model Keywords: 

Your army must include at least 1 <span class="keyword" data-term="leader" data-type="keyword">Leader</span> unit. 

1 <span class="keyword" data-term="leader" data-type="keyword">Leader</span> unit in your army must be designated as your Warlord. Note that some Factions have <span class="keyword" data-term="leader" data-type="keyword">Leader</span> units that must be your Warlord if you take them, as described in their Unit Profile under a Force Organization section.

You can include a maximum of 2 of the same units in your army for every 1,000 points, unless that unit either:
1. Possesses the <span class="keyword" data-term="battleline" data-type="keyword">Battleline</span> keyword, in which case you can take up to 2 such units for every 500 points,
or
1. Possesses the <span class="keyword" data-term="character" data-type="keyword">Character</span> or <span class="keyword" data-term="titanic" data-type="keyword">Titanic</span> keywords, in which case you can take up to 1 such unit for every 1,000 points,
or
1. Is from the Dedicated Transports unit category, in which case you can take 1 such unit for every <span class="keyword" data-term="infantry" data-type="keyword">Infantry</span> unit in your army,
or
1. Has Unit-specific conditions on the number that can be included in your army, as identified on its Unit Profile under a Force Organization section.

## Unit Rules


The Detachment you build for a Faction to suit a battle's game size is done by selecting Units. Every unit has a Unit Profile that outlines the rules for constructing that unit:
1. **Model Characteristics** for the model(s) that can be included as part of that unit.
2. **Unit Composition** in terms of number of models that can be taken in that unit.
3. **Standard wargear** that models in the unit are equipped with by default. In some instances, this section details a required *selection* between a list of wargear options that must be made. Remember that all points in Alt-hammer are additive, so Wargear costs are additional to any unit/model costs applicable to that unit, the values for which are contained in the Faction Data Tables.
4. **Special abilities** that the unit possesses and can use during a battle. 
5. **Wargear options** that the unit has access to, 
6. **Leader abilities**, if applicable, that identifies units that the unit can be attached to as a Leader.
7. **Force Organization considerations** that supersede standard Alt-hammer's standard Force Organization rules. For example, some units are limited to 1 per Detachment. In some cases, units are required to be your Warlord if your army includes that unit.

**Wargear Options** available to a unit often have limitations that are explained through specific language used in the Unit Profile. Examples of how to interpret that language are provided here:
1. *"Any model in the unit can..."* means any number of models in the unit can select options described. This does not mean *every* model in the unit has to select the upgrade.
2. *"The unit can..."* or *"Every model in the unit can..."* means the whole unit - every model - must select the upgrade described.
3. *"Up to [x] models can..."* means no more than the number of models identified by [x] can choose the upgrade described.
4. *"For every [y] models in the unit, [x] model(s) can"* is similar to above, but with the added constraint that the upgrade can only be taken when the total unit model count is met. For example, if a unit's total model count is 10, and the upgrade states that *"for every 5 models in the unit, 1 model can..."* then 2 models can take the upgrade; 1 for every 5 models in the unit of 10 models. But if that same unit's total model count is only 7, then only 1 model could take that upgrade.

Additionally, some Wargear Options will be marked with 1 or more Asterix (*). These denote additional limitations specific to that unit's ability to equip that wargear item. Those limitations are always explained directly in context in the Wargear Options section of the Unit Profile.


## Pregame Setup


Alt-Hammer games begin by determining the mission that will be played according to the **Generating a Battle** section, or by another mission design strategy agreed to by the players.

Once the Battle is generated, the following step is completed, which commences the battle:

**Determine Player Initiative for Battle Round 1:** Each player rolls a D6. The player with the highest roll takes the Initiative for the first Battle Round. Having the Initiative determines the order that the following items are completed, from highest Initiative to lowest:
1. Pre-battle Rules that apply to a player's army (ie <span class="keyword" data-term="scout-x" data-type="keyword">Scout [X]"</span> moves)
2. The Command Phase
3. The first unit Activation in Battle Round 1 

Please note that Initiative is determined separately for each Battle Round, as detailed in Phase 3 of The Battle Round.
